An Axiomatic, Mathematical Derivation of Interest Rate Dynamics

Author: Your Name

Abstract

This paper develops an axiomatic framework for interest rate dynamics based on the Kolmogorov forward equation applied to instantaneous forward rates. Starting from a minimal set of structural assumptions, we derive a class of admissible dynamics in which the forward curve evolves deterministically in maturity and stochastically in time. The resulting process admits a closed-form representation and yields tractable expressions for expectations, covariances, and variances of forward rates and conventional interest rates. A key result is that the long-time distribution of the forward rate is uniquely determined and takes a shifted inverse-gamma form, providing a direct link between the dynamic specification and its equilibrium behaviour. The model generates a consistent term structure in which mean reversion, volatility scaling, and cross-maturity dependence arise endogenously from the underlying dynamics. The framework offers a mathematically transparent alternative to standard affine term-structure models, with explicit expressions for conventional yields obtained through integration over forward maturities. This allows a clear connection between the evolution of the forward curve and observable interest rates, while preserving analytical tractability.
